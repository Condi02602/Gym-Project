Skull Crushers

The more mundane name for skull crushers is “lying triceps extensions.” This exercise is typically performed lying on your back (supine) with a barbell or dumbbells. Skull crushers work your triceps — the muscle on the back of your upper arm. The triceps, as the name implies, is a muscle with three heads.

Requirements

  • A barbell or dumbbells with appropriate weight for your fitness level
  • A clear and open space to perform the exercise
  • Comfortable and supportive workout clothes and shoes
  • Proper form and technique to avoid injury
  • Adequate warm-up and stretching before beginning the exercise
  • Gradual increase in weight and intensity as your strength and endurance improve
  • Adjustable weight machine or range of dumbbell weights for variety
  • Consistent and regular practice to see progress and results

Instructions

  • Lie on a bench or mat with your feet flat on the floor and your arms holding the weight above your chest, palms facing inward.
  • Bend your elbows and lower the weight toward your forehead, keeping your upper arms stationary and your elbows in line with your shoulders.
  • Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then use your triceps to lift the weight back up to starting position.
  • Repeat for desired number of repetitions.
  • Perform 2-3 sets of 8-12 repetitions with a weight that challenges you but allows you to maintain proper form.
  • Variations include using dumbbells, an EZ bar, or a cable machine to perform the exercise.
