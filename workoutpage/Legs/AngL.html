Angled Leg Press

An angled leg press is a type of strength training exercise that targets the lower body, specifically the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. The sled is angled at approximately 45 degrees, allowing the user to press the weight upwards at an angle rather than straight up, which can provide a slightly different stimulus to the muscles.

Requirements

  • An angled leg press machine
  • Proper workout clothing, including athletic shoes with good traction
  • A workout partner or spotter to assist with adding or removing weight plates, if necessary
  • A towel or sweatband to wipe away sweat during the workout

Instructions

  • Sit in the angled leg press machine with your back against the pad and your feet on the footplate.
  • Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the footplate with your toes pointed slightly outward.
  • Engage your core and press the footplate away from your body until your legs are fully extended but not locked out.
  • Lower the footplate back towards your body, bending your knees until they are at a 90-degree angle or slightly less.
  • Repeat for desired number of reps.
