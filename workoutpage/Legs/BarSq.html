Barbell Squats

A barbell squat, also known as a barbell back squat, is a compound exercise that activates muscle groups throughout your lower body, including your hamstrings, glutes, and lower back muscles. Perform barbell squats by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart.

Requirements

  • A barbell and weights
  • A squat rack or other apparatus to hold the barbell at the proper height
  • Proper shoes with a solid and flat sole, such as weightlifting shoes
  • A lifting belt, if desired, to provide additional support and stability to your core
  • A spotter or safety bars, if necessary, to help prevent injury in case you are unable to complete a rep

Instructions

  • Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward.
  • Grasp the barbell with both hands using an overhand grip that is slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Place the barbell on your upper back, resting it on your traps and squeezing your shoulder blades together to create a shelf for the bar.
  • Engage your core and squat down, keeping your knees in line with your toes and your weight on your heels.
  • Lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below.
  • Drive through your heels to stand back up to the starting position.
  • Repeat for desired number of reps.
