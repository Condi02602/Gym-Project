Chest Flies

The chest fly is a popular chest exercise used to develop the pectoral muscles. The exercise is performed on a bench with feet flat on the ground and knees bent at a 90-degree angle. People who are just starting their fitness journey can use a lighter weight or perform the exercise with just their body weight.

Requirements

  • Two dumbbells
  • Flat bench
  • Proper form and technique
  • Warm-up before exercise
  • Appropriate weight for your fitness level
  • Controlled movements throughout exercise
  • Breathe in when lowering the dumbbells, breathe out when pushing them up
  • Gradually increase weight and reps over time
  • Cool down and stretch after exercise

Instructions

  • Lie flat on a bench or on the floor
  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing each other
  • Extend your arms above your chest with a slight bend in your elbows
  • Lower the weights out to the sides of your body, keeping your elbows slightly bent
  • Stop when your arms are parallel to the ground
  • Pause, then bring the weights back up to the starting position
  • Repeat for desired number of reps
  • Breathe in when lowering the weights, breathe out when pushing them up
  • Use a weight that allows you to complete your reps with proper form
  • Avoid locking your elbows or letting the weights touch above your chest
  • Cool down and stretch after exercise

OR you can also use a machine instead

  • Adjust the seat height and the handles of the machine to your desired settings.
  • Sit on the machine with your back flat against the pad and your feet flat on the floor.
  • Grasp the handles of the machine and push them forward until your arms are extended in front of your chest.
  • Slowly bring the handles back toward your chest, keeping your elbows slightly bent and your back against the pad.
  • Pause when your hands are almost touching your chest and then slowly return the handles to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of reps.
