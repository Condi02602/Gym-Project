Recipe title

A typical curry from the Indian subcontinent consists of chicken stewed in an onion- and tomato-based sauce, flavoured with ginger, garlic, tomato puree, chilli peppers and a variety of spices, often including turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cardamom.

Ingredients

  • Macros Butter chicken without rice: 566, 36C, 22F, 49P
  • Chicken thigh skinless boneless 200g
  • Coconut milk 150g or coconut creme 30g + 100g water
  • Plain yogurt 100g, Onion 100g, Clove garlic 2, Piece ginger 1
  • 1 tsp turmeric, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1/4 tsp chili, 1/4 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp salt, 20 cranks black pepper
  • 50g raw rice or 250g frozen veggies

    • Instructions

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it's softened and lightly browned.
  • Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes have broken down and released their juices.
  • Add the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper (if using), and salt to taste. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the spices are fragrant.
  • Add the boneless chicken or vegetables of your choice to the pan and stir well to coat with the spice mixture.
  • Add 1/2 cup of water, cover the pan with a lid, and let the curry simmer for 20-25 minutes until the chicken or vegetables are fully cooked.
  • Serve hot with rice or naan bread, and garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
