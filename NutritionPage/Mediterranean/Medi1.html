Easy Shakshuka Recipe

A classic North African dish hailing from Tunisia, shakshuka is simple sunny side up eggs braised in a chunky tomato sauce with bell peppers and onions. Use fresh tomatoes when they are in season, but feel free to make it with canned chopped tomatoes during the winter months.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14-16 ounces)
  • 4-6 eggs
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or cilantro), chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

    • Instructions

  • Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add 1 chopped onion, 1 chopped red bell pepper, and 3 minced garlic cloves to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are soft and the onions are translucent, stirring occasionally.
  • Add 1 teaspoon of paprika, 1 teaspoon of cumin, 1/2 teaspoon of coriander, and 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper to the skillet. Stir until the spices are fragrant and well combined with the vegetables.
  • Add 1 can of diced tomatoes and their juices to the skillet, and stir well to combine with the vegetables and spices. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the tomato sauce has thickened and reduced slightly.
  • Using a spoon, make small wells in the tomato sauce and crack 4-6 eggs into the wells. Cover the skillet and simmer for another 10-12 minutes, or until the egg whites are cooked and the yolks are still runny.
  • Garnish with chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley or cilantro, and serve with crusty bread or pita to scoop up the sauce and eggs.
